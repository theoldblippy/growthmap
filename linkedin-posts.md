## Post 1 — Why Your Business Feels Stuck
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/why-your-business-feels-stuck

Most founders I talk to say some version of the same thing: "We're working harder than ever, but the results aren't matching the effort."

That feeling of being stuck is almost never about effort. It's about clarity.

When the team doesn't have a shared understanding of what drives revenue, who owns what, and what the priorities are, effort gets wasted. People are busy. Results are flat.

The fix isn't working harder. It's getting clearer on what actually matters.

## Post 2 — What Does a Fractional COO Do?
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/what-does-a-fractional-coo-do

I get asked this a lot, so I wrote it up.

A fractional COO is a part-time Chief Operating Officer who works with your business on a contracted basis. Same strategic thinking as a full-time COO. Fraction of the cost.

The short version of what they do: build the operating system, translate strategy into execution, fix what's broken in operations, and free up the founder to focus on growth.

If you've ever wondered whether your business needs one, this breaks it all down.

👉 https://growthmap.consulting/blog/what-does-a-fractional-coo-do

## Post 5 — How to Stop Being the Bottleneck
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-stop-being-the-bottleneck

If your business can't run for a week without you, that's not a badge of honor. It's a structural problem.

Most founders become the bottleneck with good intentions. You made the decisions because you were the most capable person in the room. You stayed in the weeds because nobody else knew the business like you did.

That worked at 3 people. It breaks at 10.

The fix isn't about letting go of control. It's about building decision-making frameworks, assigning ownership instead of tasks, and installing a weekly rhythm that replaces the chaos.

I wrote about the specific steps to make it happen.

👉 https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-stop-being-the-bottleneck

## Post 6 — How to Build a Weekly Operating Cadence
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-build-a-weekly-operating-cadence

The single most impactful system you can install in a growing business is a weekly operating cadence.

Same meeting. Same time. Same agenda. Every week.

The team reviews numbers, surfaces problems, and aligns on priorities. It replaces the ad-hoc Slack messages, the hallway conversations, and the 9pm emails.

After 4-6 weeks of running a tight cadence, most teams see fewer surprises, faster decisions, and Monday mornings that actually feel focused.

I wrote a step-by-step guide on how to build one.

👉 https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-build-a-weekly-operating-cadence

## Post 7 — How to Scale Without Burning Out
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-scale-without-burning-out

There's a version of scaling nobody warns you about.

Revenue goes up. Headcount goes up. Hours go up. Stress goes up. And at some point you hit a wall. Not because the business failed. Because it succeeded in a way that became unsustainable.

Most businesses scale the only way the founder knows how: more effort. But effort doesn't scale linearly. At some point, you can't work any harder.

The founders who scale sustainably aren't the ones who work the most hours. They're the ones who build businesses that don't need them in every conversation.

I wrote about how to make that shift.

## Post 8 — What Is a Business Operating System?
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/what-is-a-business-operating-system

Your business probably doesn't have an operating system. And the founder has been filling that gap with their own brain.

An operating system is just a set of repeatable structures: a meeting rhythm, a scorecard, clear ownership, and a way to resolve issues. It's not complicated. But most businesses under $10M don't have any of this documented.

The breaking point usually comes between $1M and $5M. The team gets big enough that the founder can't hold everything in their head anymore. Things start slipping.

I wrote about what an operating system actually looks like and where to start building one.

👉 https://growthmap.consulting/blog/what-is-a-business-operating-system

## Post 9 — How to Delegate When You're Used to Doing Everything
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-delegate-when-youre-used-to-doing-everything

"Nobody will do it as well as me."

Probably true. But "as well as me" is the wrong standard.

The right standard is "good enough to move the business forward without my involvement." If someone achieves 80% of what you would have done, and they do it without your time and energy, that's a win.

The real problem with delegation isn't trust. It's that most founders delegate tasks instead of ownership. "Send this email" vs. "you own the client relationship from proposal through close."

Those are completely different things. I wrote about how to make the shift.

👉 https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-delegate-when-youre-used-to-doing-everything

## Post 11 — How to Hire Your First Operations Person
**Link:** https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-hire-your-first-operations-person

The most common mistake I see: a founder hires an operations person before they can describe what they need them to do.

If you can't define the role in concrete terms, you're not ready.

The second most common mistake: hiring an admin and calling them an ops person. Those roles are valuable, but they're not the same thing. An operations person should be thinking about how the business runs, not just keeping your calendar organized.

I wrote a full guide on when you're ready, what to look for, what to pay, and how to set them up to succeed.

👉 https://growthmap.consulting/blog/how-to-hire-your-first-operations-person
